# Peer review: self-play distillation worked for 12+ generations, then degraded the policy. Recipe, not ingredients.

**Game:** Color Lines 98 (9×9, place-and-clear, stationary *survival* game; score ≈ 2.05 × turns-survived; no endgame; you die when the board fills). Deaths are early density spirals. Score is unbounded for perfect play; our best single game is ~390k, so the policy is strong-for-a-human but **nowhere near optimal** — there is large headroom.

## The setup that worked (AlphaZero-style trunk)
For ~12+ consecutive generations (pillars 2y → 2z → … → 3a → 3b) we improved the policy by:
1. Generate self-play with **NN-MCTS @ ~400 sims** (PUCT, c_puct 2.5, a value head for leaf eval, q_weight 2.0, Dirichlet+temperature exploration for state diversity).
2. **Distill the search's visit policy** back into the net: warm-start from the previous model, soft cross-entropy toward the recorded MCTS visit distribution (`train_path_b`, target_temperature 0.5; or plain T=1.0 in 2z), lr 3e-4, batch 32768, color+dihedral augmentation, ~17–40 epochs.
3. Repeat.

This is standard policy-improvement-by-distillation and it **monotonically improved** the policy for many generations. Most recent success — **pillar3b** (warm-start pillar3a, distill **V13** self-play): **+15% policy mean, floor halved** (<1000 rate 4.8% → 2.5%), val loss fell over 17 epochs.

**The teacher is a better player than the raw policy** — this has held every generation and is **not in question.** A 400-sim MCTS of the current net out-plays the raw net (as it always has). We are not asking "is the teacher better."

## The failure (current generation)
Same recipe, next generation. **V14** self-play generated by the current best policy **pillar3f** (= pillar3b + a task-arithmetic crisis vector; raw mean ~34k). Distill V14 onto pillar3f with the **exact 3b recipe** → **pillar3g**.

Result — pillar3g **regresses**, and (key new finding) the **best-val epoch (epoch 1) is already uniformly worse than pillar3f on every percentile**, then degrades further with more epochs. 1000-seed policy eval (uncapped, distribution-level — never per-seed):

| | P1 | P5 | P10 | P25 | P50 | P90 | mean | <1000 | >10k |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| pillar3f (warm-start) | 968 | 2457 | 4507 | 11142 | 24552 | 76610 | 33837 | 1.1% | 77% |
| pillar3g **epoch 1** | 786 | 2310 | 4091 | 9075 | 21298 | 72601 | 31146 | 1.7% | 72% |

Worse everywhere, including the floor. Loss curves: train falls slowly (2.294 → 2.287), **val rises from epoch 1** (2.337 → 2.352). Contrast 3b on V13: train fell to ~2.0, val fell, +15%. So **pillar3f (epoch 0) is the val-loss minimum** — any distillation of V14 moves it away (worse val, worse gameplay), uniformly, from the first epoch. (Note: in this game **val loss is a weak predictor of gameplay** — 3b gained +15% gameplay from only a ~0.004 val improvement. Floor percentiles P1–P25 are the metric that matters; means/medians are cap-pinned in capped evals.)

A separate variant, **pillar3g2** (a completed-Q / "Gumbel" improvement target instead of raw visits), regressed *harder*; its cause is understood and closed: it distilled `q_arg` = the high-value but **low-visit move the search considered and rejected** (matches the teacher's actually-played move only 2.4% of the time). That was an anti-teacher target — not the question here. The open question is the **plain visit-distillation (pillar3g)**, i.e. the proven recipe.

## Hypotheses we already KILLED with data (please don't re-suggest)
1. **"Student caught the teacher / no gap."** No — teacher still out-plays the raw policy (every generation has).
2. **"Visit distribution flattened across generations."** No — visit peakedness identical across V12/V13/V14/V15 (top-1 share 0.22, top-5-normalized).
3. **"It's the sharpening / wrong trainer."** No — pillar3g uses the *identical* trainer + T=0.5 + lr + batch as the successful 3b.
4. **"Floor-poor corpus."** No — V14's self-play floor is *higher* than V13's (stronger generator; same distribution shape, scaled up).
5. **"No learnable signal (visits ≈ prior)."** No — KL(visit-target ‖ pillar3f-prior) = **1.06** for V14 vs 0.91 for V13; there is *more* disagreement to learn in V14, not less.
6. **"Higher-entropy / noisier per-state targets."** No — sharpened target entropy identical (V13 2.095, V14 2.105).
7. **"Sims changed (200 vs 400)."** No — both 400.
8. **"Merged warm-start base (pillar3f is a task-arith merge)."** No — warm-starting from pillar3b (clean) vs pillar3f (merge) gave identical curves/result.

## The precise paradox for you
The recipe self-improved the policy for 12+ generations. The teacher still plays better. The V14 data is indistinguishable from prior generations on **every axis we've measured** (recipe, sims, base, target entropy, visit peakedness, KL-to-prior is even larger). Yet distilling V14's visit policy into pillar3f makes it **uniformly worse from epoch 1** — the visit target now points toward a *worse* policy (KL 1.06 of disagreement, but the disagreement direction degrades gameplay).

So: **ingredients fine, recipe wrong again.** (We had an analogous episode where a joint aux-loss recipe was diluting a real signal and the fix was task-arithmetic isolation — not a data change.)

Measured details that may bear on the recipe (offered as facts, not our theory):
- pillar3f's **prior is more peaked than the search's visit targets** (prior top-move share P50 0.34 vs visit target P50 0.14) — i.e. at 400 sims the search *spreads* this confident prior's mass rather than concentrating it. (For the weaker earlier models we did not measure this cleanly.)
- The policy is now ~4–5× stronger than the early successful generations (pillar3f raw mean ~34k vs 2z's raw ~7.5k).
- Self-play used `value_head_pillar3f` for leaf eval (the only generation-config difference vs V13's `value_head_pillar3a` that we have not isolated).

## Question
Given a teacher that genuinely plays better (as in all prior successful iterations), **why does distilling its 400-sim visit policy now uniformly degrade a strong student, and what change to the distillation recipe** (target construction, weighting, regularization/anchoring, what to distill, how to stop) **recovers the per-generation improvement** — without abandoning the trunk for one-off firefighting? What single controlled experiment would you run first?
